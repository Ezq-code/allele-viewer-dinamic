MORE ABOUT OUR WORK

Process developed for obtaining the information to be published

Our process is based on several stages. First, information is obtained from various related and prestigious sources that publish their scientific data on the internet. This information is structured and stored, and subsequently, cross-referencing techniques, expert criteria, and other methods are applied to guarantee its quality and veracity.

Once a sufficiently broad and reliable knowledge base is available, this information is processed to extract other data of extreme importance, such as SNPs, Allele Annotation, Allele frequency, conformation, ancestral data, age, location, color, parentage, recombination, three-dimensional backbone, etc.

This information allows us to perform 3D plotting of genes, as well as to map ancient human migrations and how they contributed to the genetic reservoir of current society. Furthermore, based on complex algorithms, we are able to evaluate the impact of diseases and medications on individuals, according to genetic studies performed.

Pharmacodynamic

Pharmacodynamics refers to the study of how drugs affect the body. It examines the mechanisms of drug action, including the relationship between drug concentration and effect, receptor interactions, and the physiological and biochemical responses produced. Understanding pharmacodynamics helps predict the efficacy and potential side effects of medications.

Pharmacokinetic

Pharmacokinetics is the study of how the body absorbs, distributes, metabolizes, and excretes drugs. It focuses on the movement of drugs within the body over time, including processes such as absorption, bioavailability, distribution, metabolism, and elimination. Pharmacokinetics is essential for determining appropriate drug dosages and schedules.

Genomic Diseases

Genomic diseases are conditions caused by alterations in an organism's DNA, ranging from single nucleotide changes to large chromosomal rearrangements.

These genetic variations can disrupt the normal function of genes, leading to a wide spectrum of health problems, including inherited disorders like cystic fibrosis or Huntington's disease, as well as complex conditions such as certain cancers and heart diseases. Understanding the specific genomic changes is crucial for diagnosis, prognosis, and the development of targeted therapies.

3D Visualization of Genes and Alleles

A New Perspective for Studying Their Origins

The understanding of genetics has advanced significantly with sequencing tools and data analysis. However, the inherent complexity of genetic interactions and the spatial organization of the genome are often lost in two-dimensional representations. Three-dimensional (3D) visualization of genes and their alleles emerges as a powerful tool that offers a revolutionary perspective, especially for unraveling the origins and evolution of these fundamental units of heredity.

Allele Viewer Tool

Enhanced Spatial Context

Visualize gene and allele location within folded chromatin, understanding positional influence on expression.

Physical Interactions Revealed

Identify physically proximate DNA regions, crucial for gene regulation and co-evolutionary insights.

Complex Allelic Relationships

Track haplotype blocks and linkage disequilibrium, fundamental for tracing the evolutionary history of alleles.

Recombination & Duplication Events

Illustrate DNA segment exchanges and duplications, showing breakpoints and new genetic configurations.

Gene Clusters & Families

Detect gene clusters from duplications, understanding how gene families formed and their functional divergence.

Interspecific Comparative Analysis

Compare synteny and chromosomal rearrangements across species, revealing events shaping genome evolution.

Mutation & Selection Simulations

Simulate allele evolution by integrating mutation rates and selective pressures to predict trajectories.

Visualization of Gene Flow

Represent gene flow between populations, showing allele dispersal and mixing, crucial for genetic diversity.
