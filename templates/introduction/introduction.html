CYP2D6 Allele Formation

"When, where, why, and how"

This page delves into the genetic history of the CYP2D6 gene, one of the most polymorphic genes in humans. With over 177 alleles identified, we explore their origins, the role of ancestral DNA, and the tools that facilitate this research, such as alleleviewer.pgxsoftware.com.

The Significance of CYP2D6 Research

As a cornerstone of pharmacogenomics, the CYP2D6 gene plays a pivotal role in the metabolism of numerous medications. Understanding its genetic variations is crucial for personalized medicine, allowing for tailored drug therapies that maximize efficacy and minimize adverse effects.

Key SNPs Inherited from Primates

From a common ancestor of orangutans and chimpanzees, humans inherited the following SNPs:

  • 180+130G>T (rs28371699)
  • 181-41T>G (rs28371702)
  • 1173+40A>C (rs1985842)
  • 1457G>C (rs1135840) S486T
  • 408G>C (rs1058164)

These SNPs are present in 78% to 67% of African individuals.

Common ancestral of Orangutan + Chimpanzee Primates:

From this ancestor, humans inherited the following SNPs:

  • 180+130G>T (rs28371699) + 181-41T>G (rs28371702) + 1173+40A>C (rs1985842) + 1457G>C (rs1135840) S486T + 408G>C (rs1058164)

From the Orangutan split we additionally inherited a stretch of DNA to later form the *2/*17 and *73 including:

  • +180+65A>G (rs1081000) +180+52G>C (rs29001518) +180+47T>C (rs1080998) +180+43C>G (rs1080997) +180+41C>A (rs1080996) +180+34G>C (rs1080995) +180+53A>C (rs28695233)

From the Chimpanzee split we additionally inherited:

  • 271C>A (rs28371703) L91M +281A>G (rs28371704) +294C>G (rs28371705) H94R +311T>C (rs76187628) V104A +319A>T (rs74802369) T107S +320C>A (rs28371706) T107N +325A>G (rs78459009) I109V +333T>C (rs1135821)

This stretch of DNA of around 70 bp will contribute to *82 and *4.012.

Allele Formation in Homo Heidelbergensis

Around 700,000 years ago, Homo heidelbergensis emerged, leading to the diversification of CYP2D6 alleles. Key allele conversions include:

  • *39ILU-Ergaster/Erectus/Ora → *2 Heidelbergensis
  • *39ILU-Ergaster/Erectus → *10 Heidelbergensis
  • *82ILU-Ergaster/Erectus/Chimp → *4.012

Migration and Allele Expansion

Between 74,000 and 11,700 years ago, human populations expanded 50-fold, leading to the spread of CYP2D6 alleles. Key events include:

  • Migration out of Africa by a small group carrying limited alleles.
  • Admixture with Neanderthals in the Middle East and Denisovans in Southeast Asia.

Pharmacogenetic Impact:

Variants of CYP2D6 influence the metabolic rate of drugs, affecting therapeutic outcomes and safety.

Global Health Relevance:

Studying CYP2D6 across diverse populations helps identify genetic predispositions to drug responses, enhancing global healthcare strategies.

Advances in CYP2D6 research drive the development of novel diagnostic tools and treatment protocols, paving the way for the future of precision medicine.
